Achievements

  • January 1961 - Wayne Gretzky is born on January 26, 1961 in Brantford, Ontario to Walter Gretzky (b. 1938) and Phyllis Hockin (b. 1941 d. 2005)

  • May 1978 - Wayne Gretzky signs with the Indianapolis Racers of the World Hockey Association (WHA) at the age of 17, since the National Hockey League (NHL) did not permit the inclusion of players under the age of 20 at the time

  • November 1978 - Wayne Gretzky is traded to the Edmonton Oilers after playing only eight games for the Indianapolis Racers

  • October 1979 - After the WHA collapsed in 1979, the Edmonton Oilers joined the NHL, after which Wayne Gretzky scored his first point

  • 1982 - Wayne Gretzky becomes the first NHL player to break the 200-point mark, after surpassing the record Maurice Richard had set of 50 goals in 50 games

  • 1984-1988 - Wayne Gretzky leads the Edmonton Oilers to four consecutive Stanley Cup wins

  • 1996 - Following an underwhelming run with the Los Angeles Kings, to whom he was traded in 1988, Gretzky was traded to the St. Louis Blues, only then to sign with the New York Rangers

  • April 1999 - Wayne Gretzky plays his final game, in which he loses to the Pittsburgh Penguins in a 2-1 overtime loss

  • November 1999 - Wayne Gretzky is inducted into the Hockey Hall of Fame
